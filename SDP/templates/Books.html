The Ramayana

By Valmiki

It is one of the two great Indian epics, traditionally attributed to the sage Valmiki. It narrates the life and adventures of Lord Rama, an incarnation of the Hindu god Vishnu, his wife Sita, and his loyal companion Hanuman.

Mahabharatha

By Vedha Vyasa

It is one of the longest epic poems in the world, traditionally attributed to the sage Vyasa. It recounts the dynastic struggle for the throne of Hastinapura between the Pandavas and the Kauravas, two branches of the royal family, culminating in the great battle of Kurukshetra.

Bhagavad Gita

By Vedha Vyasa

The Bhagavad Gita, often referred to simply as the Gita, is a sacred Hindu scripture that forms a part of the Indian epic, the Mahabharata. It is a conversation between Prince Arjuna and the god Krishna, who serves as his charioteer. Set on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, the Gita unfolds as Arjuna faces a moral dilemma about fighting in the war against his own relatives and teachers.

Mignight's Children

By Salman Rushdie

It is a novel by Salman Rushdie that intertwines the personal and political narratives of Saleem Sinai, a boy born at the stroke of midnight on August 15, 1947, the exact moment of India's independence from British rule. As Saleem's life unfolds, it becomes intertwined with the fate of his nation. Gifted with telepathic abilities, Saleem is part of a generation of children born at the same time, each possessing unique powers that reflect the diversity and tumult of post-colonial India.

A Suitable Boy

By Vikram Seth

"A Suitable Boy" is a sprawling novel by Indian author Vikram Seth, set in post-independence India during the early 1950s. The story revolves around the Mehras, a wealthy North Indian family, and follows the journey of Mrs. Rupa Mehra as she endeavors to find a suitable match for her daughter, Lata. Spanning over 1,300 pages, the novel intricately weaves together the lives of numerous characters from diverse backgrounds, reflecting the social, political, and cultural landscape of India during that period.

Untouchable

By Mulk Raj Anand

It is a novel by Mulk Raj Anand, published in 1935, which provides a stark portrayal of the life of Bakha, an "untouchable" sweeper, over the course of a single day in colonial India. Set against the backdrop of societal prejudice and discrimination, the novel highlights the dehumanizing experiences faced by those relegated to the lowest rungs of India's caste system.
